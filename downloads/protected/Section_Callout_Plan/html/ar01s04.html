4. Challenged Communications

When commercial communications are not available, the SEC will attempt to make notification on radiotelegraph and then radiotelephone immediately on notification of the incident, and then once per hour.

Radiotelegraph is far more reliable

Experience has shown that communications across the state on radiotelephone is often challenging. Stations should equip themselves for radiotelegraph communications whenever possible.
Radiotelegraph notification will occur on the hour and radiotelephone notification at 15 minutes after the hour, until such time as either the MIARPSC or QMN net has been fully activated, or until the SEC leaves for the SEOC.
Communications within the state is not always possible on the same frequency. Generally, it is straightforward to identify conditions where the standard frequencies are inappropriate.
Conditions Radiotelegraph Radiotelephone
Normal conditions 3.563 3.932
Summer, daytime, conditions of high flux (typically above 140). Lower frequencies are too noisy to support communications. Absorption on 80 meters may be high. 7.068 7.232
Winter, nighttime, low flux (typically below 90). Short skip (within state) is not possible on higher frequencies. 1.812 1.932

When the situation warrants, the DEC may also notify programs via the CMEN linked repeater system and/or the IRA link. Repeated notification, when required, will take place on the CMEN system at the bottom of the hour and the IRA system a 45 minutes after the hour.
